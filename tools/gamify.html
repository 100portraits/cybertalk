Gamification

Gamified learning is an educational approach that combines game elements with traditional learning methods to make learning more engaging and enjoyable. This approach can be highly effective in motivating learners, enhancing their retention and comprehension, and encouraging them to continue their education beyond the classroom. Gamified learning tools like these can help learners develop a passion for learning and motivate them to continue exploring new subjects and skills.

Tools we recommend:

HackShield

With HackShield, children have the opportunity to be in the shoes of cybersecurity experts (or “Cyber Agents”). Answering questions about cyber crime, children learn how to navigate the online world.

Duolingo

Duolingo is an innovative language learning platform. The app is designed in a way to make the questions playful and rewarding, while also learning valuable communication skills. Completing the lessons will earn you points to advance to a more challenging level.

Minecraft

Minecraft Education is a popular game converted to an educational tool for children. It teaches children the basics of programming, coming up with new methods for sustainable solutions against climate change, and designing accessible and inclusive spaces.

Kahoot

Kahoot is used to create multiple choice quizzes and play them in a group setting. Anyone with a smartphone or a computer can join. The player with the fastest and most correct answers will be on the top of the leaderboard.

Quizizz

Quizizz allows for creating a variety of quizzes with possibility to customize them by adding videos, photos or setting time limit or difficulty level. It has features to create Instructor or students paced classes. The teacher can also view the analytics of each students’ progress.

Classcraft

Classcraft is an online educational platform that gamifies the classroom experience and promotes student engagement. Classcraft uses an RPG (role-playing game) design to motivate students to finish assignments and participate in class. Students earn rewards and points for positive behaviour, and lose points for negative behaviours.
